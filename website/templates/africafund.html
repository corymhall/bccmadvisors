Our Purpose

Our Pan-Africa long-bias strategy offers an opportunity to invest alongside us as we seek to benefit from Africa’s emerging middle class and its rapid growth in internet penetration.

The strategy invests in publicly listed and late-stage consumer and technology-enabled businesses that exponentially enrich the lives of Africans, directly or otherwise.

Our Passion

Our lives are dedicated toward the elimination of extreme poverty in Africa.

To achieve this goal, we seek to strengthen the breadth and depth of Africa’s capital markets.

Consequently, we intentionally invest in enterprises embracing technology to raise living standards by supporting job creation, increasing financial inclusion, improving standards of education and healthcare, transforming agricultural value chains, strengthening government efficacy, and providing consumer goods and services.

Put simply, we belive in doing good through capitalism.

Our Investment Philosophy

We believe African consumer and technology-focused companies offer a compelling investment opportunity due to a unique confluence of three pillars:

  1. A dramatically improved Regulatory and Business Environment that is promoting creative destruction and unleashing humankind’s entrepreneurial spirit.
  2. An Emerging Middle Class supported by Education, Industrialization, Demographics and Urbanization.
  3. A Lollapalooza Effect propelled by mobile phone penetration and rising internet use which are driving digital innovation in Agtech, Edtech, Fintech, Healthcare, Enterprise Software, Retail and Government.

Did You Know?

Did You Know?

Here are a few facts on the continent

High-growth Cities

Africa has 86 of the 100 world’s fastest growing cities1, has as many 1 million+ people cities as Europe2 and is home to the world’s fastest growing middle-class3

A Young, Vibrant Population

60% of Africa’s population is under 25, the median age is 19 and the working-age population is expected to surpass both India and China by 20344

Massive Urban Migration

Between 2018-2025, 190 million Africans will relocate to urban centers where per capita consumption is 79% greater5

High Population Growth

Today, there are ~1.25 billion Africans. By 2050, that number is projected to increase to over 2 billion

Digital Innovation

With hundreds of millions of young, tech-savvy Africans adopting mobile handsets, Africa is leap-frogging traditional industries with novel digital products and services

Growing Internet Penetration

Internet penetration stands at 39% with the number of internet users in Africa growing by 40 million to 566 million as at end of 2019, up from 111 million in 20106

Fundamental Investors

We employ traditional scuttlebutt research and fundamental analysis to discover and invest in business models built on a strong moat.
